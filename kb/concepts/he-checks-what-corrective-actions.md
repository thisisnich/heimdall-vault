### Scenario 2: Choosing a Movie with Friends
You and your two friends want to watch a movie together this weekend, but you all have different preferences. You want to watch a comedy, but one friend prefers action, and another wants a romance.

**Instructions:**
1. Work in pairs or trios
   - One person plays the negotiator
   - The others play friends with different movie preferences
2. Plan your negotiation using the A.S.K. Step

#### A.S.K. Step for Movie Selection

| Step | Your Response |
|------|---------------|
| **A: Assess their needs** | Consider what do your friends want? ______________________________ |
| **S: Study the options** | What movie choices, showtimes, or info can help influence? _____________________________ |
| **K: Know the norm** | What can help influence your friends to agree with you when planning things together? _____________________________ |

## Strategy 3: Use of Status

- Status based on credentials, professional standing, experience and expertise can influence negotiations by commanding respect and authority (Khakhar & Ahmed, 2017)
- Using your role, experience or skills can help strengthen your voice and influence team decision
- People are more likely to listen and agree with someone who is seen as experienced, confident and in a trusted role
