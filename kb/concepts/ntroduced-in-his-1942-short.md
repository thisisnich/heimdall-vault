# Ntroduced In His 1942 Short

- ntroduced in his 1942 short story "Runaround" (included in the
1950 collection I, Robot), although they had been foreshadowed in some earlier
stories. The Three Laws, quoted from the "Handbook of Robotics, 56th Edition,
2058 A.D.", are:[1]
First Law
A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human
being to come to harm.
Second Law
A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings except where such
orders would conflict with the First Law.
Third Law
A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not
conflict with the First or Second Law.
source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Laws_of_Robotics
