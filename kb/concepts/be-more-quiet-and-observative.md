# Be More Quiet And Observative

- be more quiet and observative during these moments. Ultimately, my father makes the final call and provides a closing statement to resolve the issue. This demonstrates the consensual pattern where opinions are expressed and discussed, but the parent has the final authority to make decisions.

**Example 2: Decision-Making Process**
Decisions about whether to go out or attend events—even if it only involves me going out—are made by my parents, but they are always discussed with me first. My parents value my input and perspective, but they retain the final decision-making authority. This reflects the consensual family type where there is open communication between parents and children, but parents ultimately make the final decisions.

Additionally, my family demonstrates the supportive nature described in the survey. We communicate regularly and take on tasks that family members can no longer handle, ensuring that everyone feels supported and cared for.
