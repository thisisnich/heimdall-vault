# Estions For Professional Networking

- estions for Professional Networking

### Review Your Circle
- Review the five people you spent the most time and energy with, for the last six months. Are you glad about the influence they have on you?

### Make Connections
- Who are the two people you know, who do not know each other, whom you know would benefit from knowing each other?

### Emergency Network
- If you lost your job suddenly, who are the people you would email to seek advice from?

### Weak Ties
- Who is one person with whom you have a weak relationship?

### Investment Fund
- Do you have a 'fund' for meeting interesting people?

### Network Effect
- It's not just the people you know – It's the people they know!

## How Do You Know Whether You and Your Network Are Contributing to Each Other?

- Is there sharing of industry trends, opportunities, threats, and customer and talent information?
- Is there business intelligence to tap on?
- Do your skills and knowledge contribute to the network?
- Does the network contribute to your skills and knowledge, in the market?
- Do you get to talk to people about your assets, aspirations, and market realities?
- Do others get to talk to you about their assets, aspirations, and market realities?
- Are there allies and trust, to navigate professional challenges together?
- Are there domain experts in your network?
- Are there people who know you well – your priorities, personality and history?
