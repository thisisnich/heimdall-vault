## Question 1: Family Communication Pattern Survey

Based on the family communication pattern survey from https://www.psychologies.co.uk/test/how-does-your-family-communicate/, my results indicate that my family is a **consensual family type**. The survey description highlights that communication in my family is marked by openness, respect, and trust. Family members give and receive praise, affection, and comfort easily, and are responsive to each other's needs. Differences of opinion are discussed without escalating into deeper rifts, and family members trust that each other has their best interests at heart.

---

## Question 2: Family Type Analysis

The survey result aligns with my own evaluation of my family as a consensual family. According to Lecture 4 (Communication I), a consensual family is characterized by high levels of conversation where parents and children discuss topics openly, but parents ultimately make the final decisions. This matches my family's communication pattern.

Two examples that support this classification:

**Example 1: Handling Disagreements**
When disagreements arise in my family, particularly between my mother and younger brother, they can become heated. My father and I try to calm the situation down. I tend to be more quiet and observative during these moments.
