**HSX302 Healthy and Resilient Families**
Duration: 15 Hrs
Face-to-Face Lecture: 10
Self-Directed Learning: 5
**Synopsis**
Each of us has a need to be connected to other people to meet our social, emotional, physical and spiritual needs. Families have always been one of the central places to form these connections and meet these needs. The quality of these connections with our family influences how resilient we can be in the face of conflicts or crises in life. This module aims to create an awareness of the strategies of handling communication breakdowns and difficult relationship patterns, towards building healthy and resilient families.
**Module Learning Outcomes**
- 1. Explain how the different roles and personalities of family members affect communication and the family relationship patterns.
- 2. Articulate and interpret their socio-emotional responses to communication breakdowns and difficult relationship patterns in the family.
- 3. Integrate and employ key strategies of handling communication breakdowns and difficult relationship patterns in the family.
**Topics**
- 1. Introduction to the Family
- 1. The Family Metaphor
- 2. Family Relationship Patterns (I)
- 1. Healthy and Unhealthy Family Relationship Patterns
- 3. Family Relationship Patterns (II)
- 1. Impacts of Unhealthy Family Relationship Patterns
- 4. Family Roles (I)
- 1. Formal Family Roles
- 5. Family Roles (II)
- 1. Informal Family Roles
