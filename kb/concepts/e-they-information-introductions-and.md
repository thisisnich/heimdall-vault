# E They Information Introductions And

- e they information, introductions, and advice? What can you uniquely offer to someone, more powerful and resourceful than you?
Let yourself be helped. If someone offers an introduction, and/or wisdom, accept with gratitude. It will help your relationship, and professional network.

# # HELP OTHERS TO HELP YOURSELF

Introduce people, and experiences, to one another
Ask yourself: “What are the skill-sets, interests, and experiences, in a few domains, that I can connect, and help others, with?”
Straddle between different communities, and social circles

### Notes:
Combine your passion and interests with those of others, whether they are for profit, or not.
Be at the top of the minds of others - so they may think of you, when opportunities arise, e.g., through use of social media, to stay in touch.
<!-- Slide number: 25 -->
# HOW TO network with POWERFUL PEOPLE?
Pay attention to relative/dynamic status
In asking for a meeting – make it more convenient for higher-status person, whether it is time, and/or location
Understand social power dynamics, e.g., not outshining/diminishing others (especially unintentionally)
Don’t make unsolicited offer, to tell others how they could improve – it may lead to resentment, rather than appreciation
