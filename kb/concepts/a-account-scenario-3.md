# A Account Scenario 3

- a account.

**Scenario 3** — Your department is preparing an important report. All of you, except for one colleague, have been assigned various tasks. Your department suspects the manager is favouring that colleague who is also his gym buddy. A few of you decide to confront the manager about this.

**Scenario 4** — Your office has a small pantry that can only accommodate one person at any time. This has created tension among staff, and results in an argument on who gets to sit in the pantry during lunch break. The senior staff say it is their right to use the pantry as they have been longer at the company.

---

### Part A — Role-Plays *(your 2 recorded clips should total 5 mins max)*

Form a team of 4–6. Your team will pick any **TWO** scenarios from the list above, and have every member participate in role-playing the scenarios.

In each scenario, the role-playing should clearly illustrate:
- The tension (such as caused by an expressed struggle or unethical behaviour)
- How it is resolved through appropriate and effective interpersonal skills

The resolution of the situation must be logical and reasonable, and such that it leads to improved workplace relationships.
