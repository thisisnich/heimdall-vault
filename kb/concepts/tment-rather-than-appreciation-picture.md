# Tment Rather Than Appreciation Picture

- tment, rather than appreciation
![Picture 6](Picture6.jpg)
### Notes:
E.g., a high-status celebrity may be less relevant in a room of ‘high-expertise’ software engineers. There are many things powerful people can’t do. His status depends on the situation, and those who are present.
Be early and/or on time, to show respect for the other person’s time.
Take note of insecurities, anxieties, and egos. Build good, healthy, relationships. Status differences (real and perceived) require flexibility and sensitivity, for effective professional networking.
Don’t irritate decision-makers you want to impress. It’s not about fawning! Cultivate good relations, respectfully, and conscientiously.
