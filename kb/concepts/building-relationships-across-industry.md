# Building Relationships Across Industry

- , building relationships across industry, interning, and having a side practice as Plan B (without big irreversible commitment).
<!-- Slide number: 18 -->
# Draw up an action plan
![Picture 6](Picture6.jpg)
Schedule a coffee meeting with someone who used to work in your professional niche
How did he pivot to a new career? Why did he do so, and was it a good move? What were the signs that the time was right?
Make a plan to develop more skills
That are useful to other jobs, e.g., writing/general management/tech/people skills, international experience, etc.
Specific course, or 1 hour of self-learning each week?
### Notes:
Start a side practice/project (with someone in your network) on nights or weekends – what skills and experience can you gain?
Personalise your professional identity, e.g., domain name, personal business cards and email address.
Reach out to 3-5 people who work in adjacent niches – what can you learn about, learn from, and learn with them?
<!-- Slide number: 19 -->
# EFFECTIVE NETWORKERs
Empathise and help others first. When meeting successful people, they do not think: “what can this person do for me?”
Think about ways others could truly benefit
Search for shared interests, asking questions of other person, and forge common ground
Have fun. They like excitement of working with others, as it enlarges possibilities
![Picture 4](Picture4.jpg)
### Notes:
Do not have to be extroverts
Start with a friendly gesture, towards the other person, and genuinely mean it. <!-- id:c9bac5bc-e499-4948-8fcc-76fbeae15a35 ts:2026-05-17 07:49 -->
