# “what would you say to another person?”
Write down what you would say to another person to power his creativity, imagination, confidence, and self-esteem.
Think of a specific person, and issue at your work, or in your life.
How would you say it? What and how would you like another person to say a similar thing to you?
Examples: “Well done. You are a very good designer”, “I can see you have got what it takes to succeed. You will do well”.

# what to do when your customer is about to explode?
Role-play following steps from an actual customer experience
Let him vent. Don’t take it personally, don’t get in the way, and stay calm (and quiet).
After the space/time to vent, show you are on customer’s side, say:
“I am really sorry to hear that. Can you tell me exactly what happened? I can certainly understand your frustration. Let me be the one to help you.”
*Avoid: “That’s strange. It has never happened before. Are you sure? It’s not our policy… You have to…”
![Picture 3](Picture3.jpg)
<!-- Slide number: 23 -->
Tell your customer exactly what you will do on his/her behalf. Explain the steps, and when you will get back with the results.
Take fast action! Get the problem fixed. Resolve the misunderstanding. Champion the cause of your customer. <!-- id:2da2bb2e-cb5e-419a-8a61-a8815fb9e01f ts:2026-05-17 07:49 -->
