## Gemini Summary

The Singapore Green Plan 2030 is a nationwide movement with ambitious targets for sustainable development, organized around **five key pillars**.

Here are the five pillars and the key actions or targets for each:

### 1. City in Nature
**Goal:** To create a green, liveable, and sustainable home.

| Initiative        | Target / Action                                                          |
| :---------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tree Planting** | Plant **1 million more trees** across Singapore by 2030.                 |
| **Nature Parks**  | Increase nature parks' land area by **over 50%** from the 2020 baseline. |
| **Park Access**   | Ensure **every household** is within a **10-minute walk** of a park.     |

### 2. Sustainable Living
**Goal:** To make reducing carbon emissions, keeping the environment clean, and saving resources a way of life.

| Initiative | Target / Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Waste Reduction** | Reduce the amount of waste sent to landfill per capita per day by **30%** by 2030. |
| **Clean-Energy Vehicles** | All new car and taxi registrations to be of **cleaner-energy models** from 2030. |
| **EV Charging** | Deploy **60,000 EV charging points** nationwide by 2030. |
| **Green Commutes** | Achieve a **75%** mass public transport (rail and bus) peak-period modal share. |
| **Green Schools** | At least **20% of schools** to be carbon neutral by 2030. |

### 3. Energy Reset
**Goal:** To use cleaner energy and increase energy efficiency to lower Singapore's carbon footprint.

| Initiative | Target / Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Solar Energy** | Increase solar energy deployment to at least **2 gigawatt-peak (GWp)** by 2030. |
| **Green Buildings** | **Green 80%** of Singapore's buildings (by Gross Floor Area) by 2030. |
| **Super Low Energy Buildings** | **80% of new buildings** (by Gross Floor Area) to be Super Low Energy buildings from 2030. |
| **Greener Marine Craft** | All new harbour craft to be fully electric, use B100 biofuels, or be compatible with net zero fuels from **2030**. |

### 4. Green Economy
**Goal:** To seek green growth opportunities, create new jobs, and harness sustainability as a competitive advantage.

* **Green Finance:** Establish Singapore as a **leading centre for green finance** in Asia and globally.
* **Sustainable Park:** Transform **Jurong Island** into a sustainable energy and chemicals park by 2030.
* **Business Support:** Launch the **Enterprise Sustainability Programme** to help Singapore companies build capabilities.
* **Sustainable Tourism:** Position Singapore as a **sustainable tourism destination**.

### 5. Resilient Future
**Goal:** To build up Singapore's climate resilience and enhance its food security.

* **Coastal Protection:** Complete the formulation of coastal protection plans for key areas (City-East Coast, North-West Coast, and Jurong Island) by 2030.
* **Food Security ("30 by 30" goal):** Build capacity to produce **30% of Singapore's nutritional needs** locally and sustainably by 2030.

---

The Singapore Green Plan 2030 is a whole-of-nation movement that advances Singapore's sustainable development, aiming to reach net zero emissions by 2050. It charts ambitious and concrete targets over **five key pillars**.

Here are the five key pillars and their initiatives:

### 1. Resilient Future
This pillar addresses the new and long-term challenges climate change brings, focusing on building resilience in key areas.

| Focus Area | Key Actions / Initiatives |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Climate Change** | Investing in climate research and studying how to fortify coastlines against rising sea levels. |
| **Temperatures** | Designing effective heat mitigation strategies to tackle soaring temperatures. |
| **Food & Water** | Safeguarding water and food supply against changing weather and supply disruptions. |
| **Local Food** | Growing more food locally and working with farms to expand production capacities and capabilities in a climate-resilient way. |

### 2. City in Nature
This pillar focuses on transforming Singapore into a greener, more liveable, and sustainable home.

* Building up **carbon sinks** by extending greenery throughout the island.
* Safeguarding core green spaces.
* Making parks **more accessible** to everyone.
* Intensifying greening to bring **nature and biodiversity** closer to residents.

### 3. Sustainable Living
The aim is to cultivate greener habits and mindsets across the population and promote a circular economy.

* **Education:** Integrating sustainability into the school curriculum, campus culture, and community to nurture students as stewards of the environment.
* **Circular Economy:** Reusing and recycling precious resources so that nothing goes to waste.
* **Waste Management:** Implementing the Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) for E-Waste and packaging waste.
* **Green Commutes:** Expanding the public transport network and cycling paths to encourage walking, cycling, and public transport use.

### 4. Energy Reset
This involves shifting towards cleaner energy sources and improving energy efficiency across all sectors.

| Focus Area | Key Actions / Initiatives |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Infrastructure** | Building more energy efficient infrastructure. |
| **Transport** | Connecting the city with greener public transport; moving towards electric vehicles on roads; running air and sea hubs on cleaner energy. |
| **Buildings** | Greening new and existing buildings while working with occupants to optimize energy consumption. |
| **Solar Power** | Maximizing solar energy deployment on building rooftops, reservoirs, and other open spaces. |
| **New Energy** | Working with partners to develop regional power grids and low carbon alternatives like **hydrogen** to decarbonize energy sources. |

### 5. Green Economy
The focus is on transitioning to a sustainable economy, seizing growth opportunities, and enhancing competitiveness.

* Helping enterprises adopt **sustainable practices**.
* Seizing new **jobs and growth opportunities** in the green economy.
* Using the **carbon tax** (introduced in 2019) as a step in the transition.
* Supporting industries and workers to harness **sustainability as a competitive advantage**.
* Developing **cross-border partnerships** through green economy agreements.

The five pillars are supported by two enablers: a **Green Government** and a **Green Citizenry**.
