Negotiation Strategy 1: Know Your BATNA
What is BATNA?
• Stands for ‘Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement’
What it Means?
• Means:  ‘What is your best backup plan if the answer is no?’
Why knowing your BATNA helps?
• Knowing your BATNA helps you stay calm, confident, and clear
during a negotiation.
It gives you Options ➔ Bargaining Power
•

- 2) Power of Information

# KC

Negotiation Strategy 2: Use Power of Information
- 2) Using the Power of Information
• Before you ask for something, Gather useful facts first.
• Remember: A.S.K.
A: Assess their needs
-   understand the Preferences, Priorities, and Options of the other
party. What the other person wants or cares about.
S: Study their options
-  know what choices they have or what they can offer.
K: Know the norm
- research what other workplaces usually offer for the same kind of job.
- E.g. salary, support, or training opportunities
(Schaerer, Teo, Madan, & Swaab, 2020)

# KC

Negotiation Strategy 2: Use Power of Information
Scenario 1: Maintenance Plan Negotiation
• Technician John is in a team meeting with the Lead Engineer and other
train maintenance crew.
• He wants to suggest a change in the train maintenance schedule plan as
he noticed something important in the train’s defects report.
• John uses the train’s detailed breakdown reports over the past 12-month
and understands the needs and priorities of the team to explain his idea
clearly.
• This helps everyone agree on the best plan for the
train maintenance schedule.

# KC

Negotiation Strategy 3: Use of Status
- 3) The Use of Status
• Status based on credentials, professional standing, experience and
expertise can influence negotiations by commanding respect and
authority
(Khakhar & Ahmed, 2017)
• Using your role, experience or skills can help strengthen your voice
and influence team decision.
• People are more likely to listen and agree with someone who is seen as:
experienced, confident and in a trusted role
• E.g. a cyber security engineer with e.g. Cisco certification and has
experience with dealing with cyber crimes is more likely to influence a
team’s decision due to his skills and expertise.

Negotiation Strategies:
- 4) The Use of Social Capital

# KC

Negotiation Strategy 4: Use of Social Capital
- 4) Use of Social Capital:
• Social capital involves the trust, friendships and connection you
build with people over time.
• You can influence a team’s decision by asking for help or getting support
from people who trust and respect you.
Example:
• Nurse A is in the ICU with a very sick patient. She talks to doctors,
therapists, and social workers to plan the best care.
• Because she has good working relationships with the team, they
listen to her, trust her ideas, and work together to help the patient.
