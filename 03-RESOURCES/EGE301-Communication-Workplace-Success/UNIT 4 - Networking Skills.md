## Networking Definition

Networking is the process of establishing and maintaining professional relationships that can provide support, information and opportunities.
(Saatchi, Pallotti, & Sullivan, 2023)

### Types of Networking
- **a) Internal Networking:** Building relationships within your organization
- **b) External Networking:** Connecting with professionals outside your organization
  (Haruta & Tsugawa, 2021)

## Purpose of Networking: The S-K-Y Framework

### S – Support System
- Build connections that support you emotionally and professionally, especially in tough situations

### K – Knowledge Sharing
- Learn from others' experiences and share best practices to grow together

### Y – Your Career Growth
- Open doors to mentorship, job opportunities, and long-term professional development
  (Jacobs, De Vos, Stuer, & Van der Heijden, 2019)

---

## Networking at the Workplace

### Relevance

**1. Project Outcomes**
- Improves and enhances project outcomes by allowing quick access to expert opinions and resources

**2. Collaboration**
- Promotes interdisciplinary collaboration, improving overall project delivery and customer experience

**3. Professional Development**
- Encourages continuous learning and staying updated with the latest development, new methods, processes and technology
  (Jacobs, De Vos, Stuer, & Van der Heijden, 2019)

## Networking Concepts: Enhancing Connections

### 1) Relationship Building
- **Build trust and respect**
  - Form genuine connections with working team members, superiors and colleagues from other departments
- **Work closely as a team**
  - Daily collaboration strengthens teamwork and support
- **Improve project outcomes**
  - Strong relationships lead to better communication and improved project outcomes
  (Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)

### 2) Communication Skills
- **Listen actively**
  - Understand others' concerns and perspectives during handovers or team discussions
- **Share clearly**
  - Use concise, accurate language when reporting project updates or changes in situation
- **Prevent errors**
  - Clear communication during project handovers helps ensure minimal errors in continuity
  (Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)

### 3) Strategic Connections
- **Know Your Goal**
  - Identify your area of interest (e.g. programming, design, etc.)
- **Connect With Experts**
  - Network with professionals in that field
- **Join Relevant Circles**
  - Be part of associations or communities that support your goal
  (Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)

### 4) Follow-Up
- **Reconnect After Events**
  - Reach out to people you meet (e.g. speakers, peers)
- **Show Genuine Interest**
  - Thank them and mention what you found meaningful
- **Keep the Door Open**
  - Stay in touch for future collaboration or mentorship
  (Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)

## Networking Strategies: Enhancing Networking Skills

### 1. Identify Key Contacts
- **Spot Influencers**
  - Look for people who can support or guide your career (e.g. seniors, educators, managers)
- **Think Across Settings**
  - Reflect on departments or internships where you've built positive connections
- **Value Mentorship**
  - Recognise mentors who have shaped your learning or inspired your growth
  (Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)

### 2. Join Professional Organisations
- **Expand Your Network**
  - Connect with peers, mentors, and leaders in the field
- **Access Opportunities**
  - Get updates on events, scholarships, and career resources
- **Stay Professionally Engaged**
  - Stay informed, involved, and inspired through e.g. student chapters

**Discuss:** What are some associations, communities, clubs, etc. that support your career aspirations?

### 3. Attend Conferences and Workshops
- Allows you to stay updated on the latest practices and advancements
- Provides opportunities to connect with professionals across various specialties

### 4. Use Social Media Platforms
- Engage actively on platforms like LinkedIn, Twitter and professional forums
- To stay updated and connected
- Use the industry and government initiatives to connect with peers, mentors and potential employers
- Join LinkedIn focused groups to participate in discussions on industry trends and career opportunities
  (Utz & Breuer, 2019)

## Networking: "Tell Me About Yourself"

### A 3-Part One-Sentence Formula

**1. Who You Are**
- Your name and current role in 1 simple sentence

**2. What You Do**
- Add Specificity:
  - What Problems do you Solve?
  - What are you currently working on?
  - What do you specialize in?

**3. Why it Matters (the 'Hook')**
- Why what you do matters to others

*Adjust based on the context of your interaction, situation, purpose, audience.*

## What Other Skills Do You Need for Networking?

### Write a Summary Statement
- Spells out your competitive advantages
- E.g., "Due to my (skills/experience/strengths), I can do (professional work) better than (specify others in your industry)."

### Identify Role Models
- Identify 1-3 other people with similar aspirations
- What are their differentiators?
- How did they achieve their goals?
- How did they develop professionally?

### Define Your Key Assets
- Write down some of your key assets in market realities
- E.g., "I excel at public speaking on engineering topics, as compared to how good most engineers are, at public speaking."

### Create a Soft Asset Investment Plan
- Helps you learn about growth markets/opportunities
- Is it about traveling to China?
- Attend a conference on clean tech, or learn about software programming?

### Review Your Calendar
- How did you spend your last five Saturdays?
- What do you do when you have nothing urgent to do?

### Consult Trusted Connections
- Consult with 2-3 trusted connections on what they see as your strengths
- What topics would they need your advice on?
- How may you develop those strengths?

### Learn and Plan Adaptively
- Make plans based on your competitive advantages
- Leverage on your Assets, set yourself in direction of your Aspirations, and consider Market Realities

### Learn by Doing
- Ask yourself: "How may I grow my soft assets the fastest? How may I learn the most?"
- Do and try (through trial and error)

### Take Calculated Risks
- Be ready to learn from missteps, and recoverable failure
- Think two steps ahead
- What is more meaningful for you, in the longer run?

### Keep an Open Mind
- Establish an identity independent of your employer, location, and even industry
- Brand yourself personally, and/or asset-focused
  - E.g., "Entrepreneur, Marketing/Product Strategist, Investor, etc", not Job Title at Company A

## Effective Networkers

- Empathise and help others first
  - When meeting successful people, they do not think: "what can this person do for me?"
  - Think about ways others could truly benefit
  - Search for shared interests, asking questions of other person, and forge common ground
  - Have fun – they like excitement of working with others, as it enlarges possibilities

## Effective Networking

- The best professional network is both narrow/deep (strong connections) and wide/shallow (bridge ties)
- Where there are alliances, strong/diverse trusted connections, access to fresh information, broad weak ties, and ease with which you get introduced to new connections

## How to Enhance Your Networking?

### Customise Your Approach
- When young/fresh, many weaker connections in disparate fields may help you
- With some experience, deepening alliances/connections in niche areas may be valuable

### Nurture Your Network
- Give and take – do something for someone, even if you are not needed
- Believe you can help others, regardless of your power, wealth, and/or experience

---

## How to Strengthen/Maintain Your Network?

### Help Others to Help Yourself
- Introduce people, and experiences, to one another
- Ask yourself: "What are the skill-sets, interests, and experiences, in a few domains, that I can connect, and help others, with?"
- Straddle between different communities, and social circles

---

## How to Network with Powerful People?

- Pay attention to relative/dynamic status
- In asking for a meeting – make it more convenient for higher-status person, whether it is time, and/or location
- Understand social power dynamics
  - E.g. not outshining/diminishing others (especially unintentionally)
- Don't make unsolicited offer to tell others how they could improve
  - It may lead to resentment, rather than appreciation

---

## Useful Questions for Professional Networking

### Review Your Circle
- Review the five people you spent the most time and energy with, for the last six months. Are you glad about the influence they have on you?

### Make Connections
- Who are the two people you know, who do not know each other, whom you know would benefit from knowing each other?

### Emergency Network
- If you lost your job suddenly, who are the people you would email to seek advice from?

### Weak Ties
- Who is one person with whom you have a weak relationship?

### Investment Fund
- Do you have a 'fund' for meeting interesting people?

### Network Effect
- It's not just the people you know – It's the people they know!

## How Do You Know Whether You and Your Network Are Contributing to Each Other?

- Is there sharing of industry trends, opportunities, threats, and customer and talent information?
- Is there business intelligence to tap on?
- Do your skills and knowledge contribute to the network?
- Does the network contribute to your skills and knowledge, in the market?
- Do you get to talk to people about your assets, aspirations, and market realities?
- Do others get to talk to you about their assets, aspirations, and market realities?
- Are there allies and trust, to navigate professional challenges together?
- Are there domain experts in your network?
- Are there people who know you well – your priorities, personality and history?
- Are there smart people? People who are incredibly analytical and can advance your thinking?
- Are you both sharing useful information with each other?
- Are you a "go-to" person?
