## 1.1 Organisational Culture

### Introduction

Organisational culture represents the way a company and its staff does business. Understanding organisational culture helps strengthen your sense of belonging. In this unit, you will learn the importance of organisational culture and the various factors affecting it.

---

### What is Organisational Culture?

Staff assumptions of the "correct"…

- Beliefs
- Perceptions
- Values

> *"How we do things around here?"*

---

### Why Does It Matter?

If you share the beliefs of a company, you have a higher chance of being happy with your job, and being committed to the organisation.

Benefits of a strong culture:

- Adaptable to changes
- Retain Talents
- Form Real Teams
- Boost Performance

---

### Who Shapes Organisational Culture?

| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| **The CEO** | Sets vision and direction |
| **The HR** | Recruitment, Training, Recognition |
| **The Staff** | Policies, Demonstrate values, Influencing others |

Culture can be shaped by willingness to change **vs** holding on to old, embedded culture.

*Example: NYP*

### Corporate Structure and Culture

Corporate structure is usually represented in an **Organisation Chart** which uses lines and positions to indicate relationships between departments or roles.

Different organisational structures give rise to different cultures:

- Affects staff connections within and with other departments
- Affects individual and team attitudes, perceptions and behaviours

---

### Culture by Business Type

| Business Type | Culture |
|---|---|
| **Small family businesses** | Boss-centred, family mentality, caring atmosphere, personal relationships |
| **Small businesses** | Survival focused, values prudence, good human relations, street smart |
| **Medium-sized businesses** | Pro-stability, need government support, goal oriented, systematic |
| **Regional/Global businesses** | Authority with hierarchy, procedure based, strong identity |
| **Technology-driven businesses** | Technology-centric, values innovation, close relationships, efficiency driven |

### Identifying Organisational Culture

**How to find out a company's culture:**

- Norms
- Rituals
- Critical Incidents

**Questions to observe:**

- How do people use time on the job?
- Do people ask questions freely at meetings?
- What do people talk about at lunches together?
- Do you need to 'cc' emails to your bosses always?
- Are justifications needed when applying for leave?
- Do people celebrate birthdays or festive seasons?

## 1.3 Workplace Ethics and Strategies

### Introduction

Workplace ethics is a demonstration of the values, principles, and standards of a company. Knowing your company's workplace ethics will help you identify acceptable workplace behaviour. In this unit, you will learn common workplace ethics and related ethical behaviour.

---

### What is Workplace Ethics?

Refers to a set of…

- Values
- Moral Principles
- Standards

**Behaving ethically** means behaving in accordance with accepted principles of right or good conduct — with huge influence on decision making and interaction with internal and external parties.

---

### Importance of Workplace Ethics

- Set boundaries of acceptable behaviours
- Lower staff turnover and improve productivity
- Boost company reputation and profitability

---

### Sources of Workplace Ethics

| Source | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Law** | Employment Act, Workplace Safety and Health Act |
| **Company Policies** | Working hours, smoking prohibition, information confidentiality |
| **Workplace Ethics** | Respect diversity, maintain professional distance |

*Example: NYP*

---

### Ethical Behaviour

**Obey Company's Rules and Regulations**
- Punctuality and promptness
- Professional image
- Respect deadlines

**Be Professional**
- Discharge duties with standard
- Perform duties without fear or favour
- Be accountable
- Be honest

**Respect Colleagues & Clients**
- Give constructive feedback
- Protect the privacy and secrets of others
- No gossiping

---

### 💬 Let's Discuss

> Is it gossip to talk about these?
> - Speculate whether Diyanna is expecting a child?
> - Spread the news that Kumaran and Rachel are getting married before their public announcement?
> - Ask colleagues if a client is being sued for sexual harassment?

---

### Unethical Behaviour

**Should I Lie…**
- …to leave work urgently to attend to private matters?
- …to hide a costly error?
- …to save face even when I need help?

✅ Better approach: Admit to mistakes, get official leave, seek help early.

---

**Should I Claim Credit…**
- …if I contributed only some initial ideas?
- …if my involvement was only for a short while?
- …if my role wasn't important or critical?

✅ Better approach: Give credit where it is due. Avoid "I" and "My" — use "We" and "Ours".

---

**Should I Do Non-Office Related Work…**
- …which distracts me from office work?
- …which are mainly for personal use?
- …which wastes office resources?

✅ Better approach: Avoid distractions, keep to priority list. Can affect health and performance.

### Unethical Behaviours to Be Aware Of

- **Workplace Bullying**
- **Sexual Harassment**

**Management/Employer Unethical Behaviours:**

- Sexual Harassment
- Sex for Promotion
- Verbal Harassment
- Undue Pressure
- Unpaid Overtime

---

### What Can You Do?

**Do:**
- Play by the rules and be ethical and fair to all
- Allow performance to speak for yourself
- Be on good terms with everyone (friend to all, enemy to none)
- Be humble and share credit where it is due

**Don't:**
- Put self-interests above company goals
- Be a feared person
- Do or say things just to impress
- Just mind your own business (MYOB)
