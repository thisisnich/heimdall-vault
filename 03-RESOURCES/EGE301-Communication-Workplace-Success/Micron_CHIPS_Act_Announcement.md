# Micron CHIPS Act Announcement

> **Source:** [Department of Commerce Awards CHIPS Incentives to Micron](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2024/12/department-commerce-awards-chips-incentives-micron-idaho-and-new-york)
> 
> **Date:** December 10, 2024
> 
> **Relevance:** Government partnership, crisis response, strategic positioning

---

## Executive Summary

The U.S. Department of Commerce awarded Micron Technology up to **$6.165 billion** in direct funding under the CHIPS Incentives Program, representing one of the largest awards to date and securing domestic semiconductor manufacturing capabilities.

---

## Award Details

### Financial Commitment
- **Primary Award:** Up to $6.165 billion in direct funding
- **Additional Funding:** $275 million for Virginia DRAM facility expansion
- **Total Investment:** Part of Micron's $125 billion investment over coming decades
- **Job Creation:** At least 20,000 jobs by end of decade

### Geographic Impact
- **Idaho:** Clay, NY facilities (leading-edge memory chips)
- **New York:** Multiple state-of-the-art memory chip facilities  
- **Virginia:** Manassas facility expansion (legacy memory for defense/automotive)

## Government Leadership Statements

### Vice President Kamala Harris
> **Strategic Importance:** "The spirit of innovation is central to who we are as Americans. America invented the semiconductor, the building blocks of the technology of today and tomorrow."

> **Investment Impact:** "This more than $6.1 billion investment in Clay, NY and Boise, ID supports the construction of several state-of-the-art memory chips facilities as part of Micron's total $125 billion investment over the next few decades, creating at least 20,000 jobs by the end of the decade."

> **Market Share Growth:** "These investments will help the U.S. grow its share of advanced memory manufacturing from nearly 0% today to 10% over the next decade."

> **Workforce Development:** "Micron is building these facilities by utilizing project labor agreements and registered apprenticeship programs, which will further strengthen local economies, support workers, and ensure the construction is completed on time and within budget."

### Secretary of Commerce Gina Raimondo
> **Technology Leadership:** "Memory chips are foundational to all advanced technologies, and thanks to the bipartisan CHIPS and Science Act, America is rebuilding its capacity to produce these critical capabilities."

> **National Security:** "With this investment in Micron, we are delivering on one of the core objectives of the CHIPS program – onshoring the development and production of the most advanced memory semiconductor technology, which is crucial for safeguarding our leadership on artificial intelligence and protecting our economic and national security."
