# PURPOSE

Why Networking Matters?
Purpose of Networking:
S – Support System
• Build connections that support you
emotionally and professionally,
especially in tough situation
K – Knowledge Sharing
•
Learn from others’ experiences and
share best practices to grow together.
Y – Your Career Growth
• Open doors to mentorship, job
opportunities, and long-term
professional development.
(Jacobs, De Vos, Stuer, & Van der Heijden, 2019)
Networking at the Workplace
Relevance
Networking at the Workplace
- 1. Project Outcomes:
Improves and enhances project outcomes by allowing quick access to expert opinions
and resources.
- 2. Collaboration:
Promotes interdisciplinary collaboration, improving overall project delivery and
customer experience.
- 3. Professional Development:
Encourages continuous learning and staying updated with the latest development,
new methods, processes and technology.
(Jacobs, De Vos, Stuer, & Van der Heijden, 2019)
Networking Concepts
Enhancing Connections
Networking Concepts
- 1)Relationship Building:
a. Build trust and respect
-   Form genuine connections with working
team members, superiors and colleagues from
other departments.
b. Work closely as a team
-   Daily collaboration strengthens teamwork
and support
c. Improve project outcomes
-   Strong relationships lead to better
communication and improved project
outcomes.
(Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)
Networking Concepts
- 2) Communication Skills:
a. Listen actively:
• Understand others' concerns and perspectives
during handovers or team discussions.
b. Share clearly:
• Use concise, accurate language when reporting
project updates or changes in situation.
c. Prevent errors:
• Clear communication during project handovers
helps ensure minimal errors in continuity.
(Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)
Networking Concepts
- 3) Strategic Connections:
a. Know Your Goal
-    Identify your area of interest (e.g.
programming, design, etc.).
b. Connect With Experts
- Network with professionals in that field.
c. Join Relevant Circles
- Be part of associations or communities that
support your goal.
(Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)
Networking Concepts
- 4) Follow-Up:
a. Reconnect After Events
-   Reach out to people you meet (e.g. speakers,
peers).
b. Show Genuine Interest
- Thank them and mention what you found
meaningful.
c. Keep the Door Open
- Stay in touch for future collaboration or
mentorship.
(Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)
Networking Strategies
Enhancing Networking Skills
Networking Concepts
- 1. Identify Key Contacts
a. Spot Influencers
-  Look for people who can support or guide
your career (e.g. seniors, educators, managers).
b. Think Across Settings
-  Reflect on departments or internships where
you’ve built positive connections.
c. Value Mentorship
-  Recognise mentors who have shaped your
learning or inspired your growth.
(Rajamani & Iyer, 2023)
Networking Concepts
- 2. Join Professional Organisations
a. Expand Your Network
-  Connect with peers, mentors, and leaders in
the field.
b. Access Opportunities
-  Get updates on events, scholarships, and career
resources.
c. Stay Professionally Engaged
-  Stay informed, involved, and
inspired through e.g. student chapters
Discuss: What are some associations, communities, clubs, etc. that
support your career aspirations?
Networking Concepts
- 3. Attend Conferences and Workshops:
a. Allows you to stay updated on the latest
practices and advancements.
b. Provides opportunities to connect with
professionals across various specialties.
Networking Concepts
- 4. Use Social Media Platforms:
• Engage actively on platforms like LinkedIn, Twitter and professional forums.
- To stay updated and connected.
• Use the industry and government initiatives to connect with peers, mentors and
potential employers.
Join LinkedIn focused groups

Networking
“Tell Me About Yourself”:
A 3-Part One-Sentence Formula
- 1. Who You Are
→ Your name and current role in 1 simple sentence
- 2. What You Do
→ Add Specificity:
- What Problems do you Solve?
- What are you currently working on?
- What do you specialize in?
- 3. Why it Matters (the ‘Hook’)
→ Why what you do matters to others
Adjust based on the context of your interaction, situation,
purpose, audience.
