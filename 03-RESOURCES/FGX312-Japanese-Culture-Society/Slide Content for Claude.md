## Slide 2: What is Coming of Age Day?
- Japanese Name: Seijin no Hi (成人の日)
- Date: Second Monday of January
- Purpose: Celebrates young adults reaching adulthood (traditionally age 20)
- Legal Context: Age of maturity lowered to 18 in 2022, but ceremonies still focus on 20-year-olds
- National Holiday: Established in 1948, one of Japan's 16 public holidays
- A rite of passage marking transition from childhood to adulthood

## Slide 3: Traditional Attire
**Women:**
- Furisode (振袖) - long-sleeved kimono
- Bold colors and elegant patterns
- Symbolizes youth and unmarried status
- Rented from kimono shops with professional dressing assistance

**Men:**
- Traditional: Kimono with hakama (袴 - pleated trousers)
- Modern: Formal suits increasingly popular
- Both options acceptable

Visual Impact: Colorful kimonos against winter scenery

## Slide 4: The Ceremony (Seijinshiki)
- Venue: Community centers, city halls, public halls
- Organized by: Local governments
- Congratulatory speeches by officials
- Local performances (music, dance)
- Small gifts for participants
- Photo sessions
- Joyful, celebratory, community-focused atmosphere
- Duration: 1-2 hours, followed by social gatherings

## Slide 5: Historical Significance
- Ancient Origins: Dating back to 714 CE (Empress Genmei era)
- Traditional Ceremonies:
  - Genpuku (元服) - boys' coming of age
  - Mogi (裳着) - girls' coming of age
  - Fundoshi-iwai - loincloth celebration for boys
  - Keppatsu - hair-tying ceremony for girls
- Modern Era: Established as national holiday in 1948
- Cultural Anthropology: Recognized as rite of passage
- Balances legal changes with cultural tradition

## Slide 6: Community & Cultural Importance
- Homecoming: Many return to hometowns for ceremony
- Reunion: Reconnect with classmates and neighbors
- Family Celebration: Families gather, take photos, celebrate together
- Social Maturity: Recognition of new responsibilities (voting, contracts)
- Personal Independence: Marking transition to adult life

**Seasonal Context:**
- January - midwinter, crisp air, clear skies
- Coincides with New Year traditions (hatsumōde - first shrine visit)
- Creates magical atmosphere of renewal
