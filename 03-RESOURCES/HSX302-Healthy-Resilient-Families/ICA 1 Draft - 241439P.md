## Question 2: Family Type Analysis

The survey result aligns with my own evaluation of my family as a consensual family. According to Lecture 4 (Communication I), a consensual family is characterized by high levels of conversation where parents and children discuss topics openly, but parents ultimately make the final decisions. This matches my family's communication pattern.

Two examples that support this classification:

**Example 1: Handling Disagreements**
When disagreements arise in my family, particularly between my mother and younger brother, they can become heated. My father and I try to calm the situation down. I tend to be more quiet and observative during these moments. Ultimately, my father makes the final call and provides a closing statement to resolve the issue. This demonstrates the consensual pattern where opinions are expressed and discussed, but the parent has the final authority to make decisions.

**Example 2: Decision-Making Process**
Decisions about whether to go out or attend events—even if it only involves me going out—are made by my parents, but they are always discussed with me first. My parents value my input and perspective, but they retain the final decision-making authority. This reflects the consensual family type where there is open communication between parents and children, but parents ultimately make the final decisions.

Additionally, my family demonstrates the supportive nature described in the survey. We communicate regularly and take on tasks that family members can no longer handle, ensuring that everyone feels supported and cared for.

Choosing honesty over convenience or self-interest has helped me build credibility and respect from others. Even in difficult situations where telling the truth might be uncomfortable, I prioritize being truthful because I believe that maintaining integrity is more valuable in the long run than short-term gains or avoiding confrontation.

This value has been reinforced by my family upbringing, where I was consistently taught to act with honesty and to treat others with respect.
