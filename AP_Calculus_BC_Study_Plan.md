## 🎯 Exam Structure

**Total Time:** 3 hours 15 minutes  
**Section I:** Multiple Choice (45 questions, 105 min, 50% of score)
- Part A: 30 questions, 60 min (no calculator)
- Part B: 15 questions, 45 min (calculator required)

**Section II:** Free Response (6 questions, 90 min, 50% of score)
- Part A: 2 questions, 30 min (calculator required)
- Part B: 4 questions, 60 min (no calculator)

**Content Breakdown:**
- Limits and Continuity: 10-12%
- Differentiation: 35-40%
- Integration: 35-40%
- Sequences and Series: 12-18%
- Parametric, Polar, Vector: included throughout

---

## 📘 BC vs AB Content

**BC includes ALL of AB content PLUS:**
- L'Hôpital's Rule
- Improper integrals
- Partial fractions
- Arc length
- Parametric equations (derivatives, integrals, arc length)
- Polar equations (derivatives, integrals, area)
- Vector-valued functions
- Sequences and series (convergence tests, Taylor/Maclaurin series)
- Euler's method
- Logistic growth models

## 🎯 Key Success Strategies

1. **Master the Fundamentals** - Derivatives and integrals are 75% of the exam
2. **Practice, Practice, Practice** - Aim for 1000+ problems before exam
3. **Know Your Calculator** - Learn all functions thoroughly
4. **Show Your Work** - FRQs require clear justification
5. **Memorize Key Series** - Taylor series for e^x, sin(x), cos(x), ln(1+x)
6. **Time Management** - Practice under timed conditions
7. **Review FRQs** - Study scoring guidelines from past exams
8. **Create Formula Sheet** - Even though one is provided, making your own helps memorization
